01 · The daily toolkit

Apps You Need

The handful of apps that actually get used week to week, with what each one is for and where to get it.

Banking & Finance
N26 n26.com

A fully digital German bank account, opened from the phone in about 10 minutes with just a passport — no branch visit needed. It gives a proper German IBAN, which is what landlords, the university, insurers, and mobile providers will ask for when setting up direct debits (Lastschrift).

  • Free virtual Mastercard immediately, physical card arrives by post in a few days
  • Free withdrawals at most ATMs within the Eurozone on the standard account
  • Instant spending notifications and a built-in "Spaces" feature for setting money aside (e.g. rent, travel)
  • English-language app throughout — no German required to use it day-to-day
Wise wise.com

For sending money between India and Germany — usually noticeably cheaper than a regular bank transfer, with the real mid-market exchange rate and a small, transparent fee shown upfront before sending.

PayPal paypal.com

Still widely accepted at German and international online stores, and an easy way to send or split money with friends without sharing bank details.

Shopping & Daily Life
Amazon Shopping amazon.de

For anything not worth hunting down in person — electronics, textbooks, and everyday essentials, usually with fast delivery across Germany.

Kleinanzeigen kleinanzeigen.de

Germany's biggest second-hand marketplace (formerly eBay Kleinanzeigen) — the normal way students furnish a room cheaply and find a decent used bicycle, often for a fraction of retail.

REWE rewe.de

Online grocery ordering from one of Germany's biggest supermarket chains, with regular discounts and a loyalty-offers section worth checking before a big shop.

Lidl Plus lidl.de

Free loyalty app for Lidl — personalized coupons and small cashback offers that add up to real savings over a couple of years of grocery runs.

Food Delivery
Lieferando lieferando.de

Germany's largest food delivery app — the default choice for ordering from local restaurants, including most of the Indian places listed further down.

Too Good To Go toogoodtogo.de

Leftover food from bakeries, cafés, and supermarkets sold at 50–80% off to stop it going to waste — a genuinely good way to save money on food regularly, not just occasionally.

Flink goflink.com

Grocery delivery in as little as 10–20 minutes in supported cities — milk, bread, fruit, vegetables, snacks, drinks, and household basics, without leaving the desk mid-assignment.

  • Genuinely useful on the days packed with classes, assignments, or a part-time shift
  • Frequent discount codes for first-time users — worth checking before the first order
Transport & Navigation
DB Navigator bahn.de

The official Deutsche Bahn app and the one to actually own for train travel — booking ICE (high-speed), IC, RE (regional), and S-Bahn trains, all in one place.

  • Real-time delay alerts and platform (Gleis) info, which changes more often than you'd expect
  • Digital tickets with QR code — no printing needed
  • Also shows connections that combine trains with local buses/trams for the full door-to-door route
Deutschlandticket App bahn.de

Manages the Deutschlandticket subscription directly — activating it each month, storing the digital ticket, and showing which trains/buses/trams it covers nationwide (regional and local transport, not high-speed ICE/IC).

Google Maps maps.google.com

The essential everyday app — walking/driving navigation, live traffic, and public transport routing that works well even in a smaller city like Ingolstadt.

Citymapper citymapper.com

Not needed daily in Ingolstadt itself, but noticeably better than Google Maps for public transport in Germany's bigger cities — worth having installed before a weekend trip to Berlin, Munich, or Hamburg.

FlixBus flixbus.com

A long-distance coach network that connects Ingolstadt to dozens of German and European cities — usually the cheapest way to get somewhere far, especially booked a few weeks ahead.

  • Buses depart from Ingolstadt Nordbahnhof and the ZOB (central bus station)
  • Free WiFi and power outlets on board; one carry-on and one stored bag included
  • Digital ticket with QR code — no printing needed, just show it on the app
  • Good for weekend trips, or for visitors flying into a bigger hub and busing the rest of the way
Health & Bureaucracy
Your insurer's app (e.g. TK) tk.de

Whichever public health insurer is used, their app is where the digital insurance card lives and where medical receipts get uploaded for reimbursement. TK in particular runs a 24/7 English-language support line, which is worth knowing about before it's needed.

Doctolib doctolib.de

The easiest way to find and book a doctor's appointment online, without needing to call and speak German. Search "Ingolstadt" and filter by specialty — the profile for each doctor lists the languages they speak, so filtering or scanning for "English" surfaces GPs, dentists, and specialists who can consult in English nearby.

  • Appointments can usually be booked directly online, no phone call needed
  • Useful for registering with a Hausarzt (general practitioner) soon after arriving, before one is urgently needed
  • Also covers dentists, gynaecologists, dermatologists, and most specialists
Housing & Everyday Life
WG-Gesucht wg-gesucht.de

The main platform in Germany for shared flats (WG) and single student rooms. Listings can be filtered by city, price, move-in date, and whether the room is furnished — most serious landlords and flatmates post here first.

  • A short, friendly self-introduction message gets far more replies than a generic one
  • Some listings ask for a Schufa credit report — not usually expected in the first year as a student, but worth asking about upfront

Catholic Uni in DE

KU is Germany's only Catholic university, and personal faith plays no part in admissions — it's fully accredited and equal in standing to state universities. It runs two campuses: the historic main campus in Eichstätt, and the Ingolstadt campus, home to the WFI – Ingolstadt School of Management, one of Germany's better-known business schools.

Ingolstadt itself is a mid-size Bavarian city on the Danube, about an hour north of Munich, and is best known as the headquarters of Audi — which means plenty of part-time and internship opportunities alongside the degree. The compact campus, close-knit student community, and low semester fees (which usually bundle in a public transport ticket) make it an easy place to settle into.

03 · Trains, buses, and the airport

Getting Around

Local & regional

VGI Network

VGI (Verkehrsverbund Großraum Ingolstadt) is the transit authority covering Ingolstadt city buses plus the wider Eichstätt, Neuburg-Schrobenhausen, and Pfaffenhofen region under one fare system. The VGI app handles timetables with live delay info, door-to-door route planning (including the walk to the stop), and e-ticket purchase — pay by SEPA direct debit, card, PayPal, or Google/Apple Pay. It also books "VGI Flexi," an on-demand shuttle for areas buses don't reach directly.

vgi.de →
Nationwide subscription

Deutschlandticket

A flat monthly subscription (around €63/month, often discounted for students through the semester contribution as a "Semesterticket") for unlimited local and regional transport anywhere in Germany — buses, trams, and regional trains, though not high-speed ICE/IC trains. It's issued and managed as an e-ticket inside the VGI app and can be cancelled month to month.

To the airport

Ingolstadt Airport Express

A direct coach running roughly once an hour between Ingolstadt and Munich Airport (MUC) — no need to route through Munich's city trains just to catch a flight. Tickets are bought the same way as any VGI ticket, through the app or at the counter, and it's covered under the wider VGI fare system.

04 · Weekends worth having

Explore Ingolstadt & Beyond

Landmark

Kreuztor

The red-brick medieval gate that's become the city's unofficial symbol — best seen in early morning light.

Museum

Audi Forum & Museum Mobile

Factory tours plus a museum tracing Audi's history through around a hundred cars and motorcycles.

Church

Maria de Victoria (Asamkirche)

A small Rococo church with a ceiling fresco painted to trick the eye into seeing a dome that isn't there.

Old Town

Neues Schloss & Bavarian Army Museum

A 15th-century castle with an ornate courtyard, now home to the region's military history collection.

Riverside

Klenzepark & the Danube

Riverside paths, green space, and the easiest spot in town for a quiet walk or a picnic between classes.

Shopping

Ingolstadt Village

A large designer outlet just outside the city — a popular weekend trip for visiting friends.
